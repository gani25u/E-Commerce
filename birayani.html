Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent as a variety of Persian pilaf. It is made with Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat (such as chicken, beef, lamb, or fish), and sometimes eggs or potatoes.

There are many regional and cultural variations of biryani, with each region having its own unique twist on the dish. Some popular types include Hyderabadi biryani, Lucknowi biryani, and Kolkata biryani.

In addition to the traditional meat-based versions, there are also vegetarian biryanis that use vegetables and paneer instead of meat. Biryani is often served with raita (a yogurt-based side dish) and salad.
